Objectives

Moore’s law has been the golden law to predict the semiconductor device size and guide the long-term planning of semiconductor industry in the last 3 decades. It has been accuractely describing a driving force behind technological and economical growth.

This post is to visualize Moore’s law in semiconductor manufacturing.

Work flow

Main work flow to construct motion chart of Moore’s law require following steps:

  1. Scape data from credible sources
  2. Formate data for motion chart
  3. Generate motion chart with GoogleVis package
  4. Deploy to website with blogdown

Solution

Review of Prior Art

Moore’s law predicted that chip performance would double every 18 months, a driving force to shrink the device constantly over the time.

The data source of this report would be parsed from Wiki pages, which are reliable and consistent with other published results: Moore’s Law, [device dimension] (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor_device_fabrication) and transistor counts.

The data can be scrapped from html tables directly from R packages. This report start with manual construction of data table from above source.

Motion chart was first demonstrated by Hans Rosling. The chart is made possible by GoogleVis package.
